Active
Operation Type: Specific / Test
Drone: Custom Quadcopter w/ 800W Fuel Cell
Location: Segregated Area (Kjeller/Felt)

ID Hazard Cause Effect Initial Preventative Barriers Recovery Measures Residual
GENERAL
H-01 Lithium Battery Fire/Explosion Batteries not stored at storage voltage. Overheating during charging. Escalating fire. 3-C
  • Procedures implemented for inspecting batteries before flight
  • Storing at correct voltage
  • Supervised charging
  • Transport and store batteries in LiPo safe bags.
  • Class D fire extinguisher available.
 4-D
H-02 Unsuccessful Launch/Landing Pilot error, Wind, Wrong mounted propellers. Crash and hits persons. Battery fire escalating to a forest fire. 2-C
  • Pilot training.
  • Stabilize mode always used.
  • Wind limitations implemented.
  • Weather checked before takeoff.
  • Operations only over a controlled ground area.
  • Min safe distance 200m horizontally from uninvolved.
  • Do not fly in high forest fire risk.
 4-D
LOSS OF CONTROL
H-03 Loss of Command Link RC power runs out, inadvertently turned off, or receiver cable loose. Loss of ability to control. 3-C
  • Check power on transmitter before flight.
  • Transmitter button design (guarded/press-and-hold).
  • Preflight inspection.
  • Flight controller failsafe (RTH).
  • Loiter 5s then autoland.
  • Max altitude 4m AGL over level terrain.
 4-D
CONTAINMENT & COLLISION RISKS
H-04 Airspace violation Fly away due to FC error. Near mid air collision or mid-air collision. 1-C
  • Standard procedures for checking airspace.
  • Operate min 5 km from airports.
  • Reliable COTS flight controller.
  • Map nearby airspace.
  • ATC phone number in ERP.
  • Notify ATC if fly-away occurs.
 4-D
H-05 Mid-air Collision Low flying aircraft entering the airspace. Crash / Fatality. 1-C
  • Max altitude 4m AGL (below tree top).
  • Check area far from airfields/helipads.
  • Pilot lands immediately if aircraft detected.
  • None (Collision at 4m is catastrophic but extremely unlikely if barrier holds).
 1-E
HYDROGEN FUEL CELL RISKS
H-06 Tank rupture or hydrogen leak Hard landing or crash. Hydrogen explosion / Fire. 2-C
  • Use certified tank (pressure tested yearly).
  • Pilot training (smooth landing).
  • Altitude restriction 4m AGL.
  • Protective structure around tank.
  • Min safe distance 300m.
  • Post-crash: Keep clear 1 hour / Vent check.
  • Limit pressure to 150 bar (Incremental test).
 4-D
H-07 Hydrogen leak (System) Valve or seal failure. Gas pockets in fuselage igniting. 2-B
  • Hydrogen gas detector monitoring.
  • Pressure sensor alert (unexpected drop).
  • Inspect seals/hoses pre/post flight.
  • Leak check (sniffer/spray).
  • Ventilation holes in fuselage.
  • Well ventilated storage.
 4-D
H-08 Fuel cell failure Complex failure modes. Loss of power leading to crash. 2-B
  • Back-up batteries for landing (Hybrid system).
  • Altitude restriction 4m AGL reduces impact energy.
 4-D
H-09 Freezing water/air damage Sub-zero ops. Moisture freezing in cell/valves. Fuel cell/Valve/Seal failure. H2 Leak. 2-B
  • Use FC approved for sub-zero.
  • Low-temp seals.
  • Store cool/dry (avoid condensation shock).
  • Purge procedure.
  • Backup batteries.
  • Inspect for ice/leaks pre/post flight.
 4-D
H-10 Ignition of H2 indoors Static electricity / Sparks. Fire/Explosion in workshop. 3-B
  • Ground drone (anti-static strap).
  • No open flames.
  • Well ventilated room.
  • Daily leak checks.
 4-D
